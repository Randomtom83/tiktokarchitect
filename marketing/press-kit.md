# Press Kit — Tom Reynolds / TikTokArchitect

For interview requests or fact verification: **Tom@TikTokArchitect.com**.

## The short version

Tom Reynolds is an architectural designer in New Jersey. He has 18+ years in practice. He spent nearly a decade at John J. Perkins, AIA on Mack-Cali commercial work. He runs Green Stories LLC. He runs NIA, the Black Architecture Archive, at <https://nia.greenstoriesllc.com>. He has built 22 free professional tools for architects, engineers, and contractors. He posts as @TikTokArchitect, where he has published more than 1,000 short videos about architecture.

He is on the path to becoming one of about 2,500 Black male architects practicing in the United States, in a profession with fewer than 3,000 Black architects in total. He is not yet licensed. The site, the videos, the tools, and the archive are how he is working on that — his and the rest of the field's.

## The longer version

Tom Reynolds graduated NJIT in 2008 with a Bachelor of Architecture. He started his career at John J. Perkins, AIA, where he spent close to 9 years as a lead designer on commercial office and warehouse work for Mack-Cali — financial-brand offices, non-profit fitouts, a test kitchen for a national brand. He then worked at MBA Architects on historic restorations, where his projects took the Bricks & Mortar Preservation Award. He has been a designer at NK Architects, Peter Gisolfi Associates, and InfiniRE. He was a partner at Neighborhood Strategies. He is now principal at Green Stories LLC.

He is Associate AIA, LEED Green Associate, OSHA 30, and FEMA Hurriplan certified. He uses LiDAR and drone photogrammetry to capture existing site and building conditions. He has held leadership positions in NJ NOMA — co-founder, parliamentarian, currently VP of the New Jersey chapter.

He posts as **@TikTokArchitect**. Over 1,000 videos. The TikTok work is education. Sometimes a code-compliance walkthrough, sometimes a tool demo, sometimes the actual experience of being a Black designer in a profession that defaults him out of recognition. He has been asked, on the record by a subcode official, "you are the architect?" with the inflection on "you". A client once called the police on him for entering their own vacant property.

He has built **22 free professional tools** — egress width, fire separation, FAR/lot coverage, fixture counts, occupant load, plumbing pipe sizing, joist selection, footing sizing — and a Revit-to-3MF exporter called **AnansiBuild** *(currently in private beta)* that supports 26 3D printers and 19 architectural scales. He built **Architect the Game**, an Android game about the choices that shape an architectural career. First all-nighter to ARE. Free. Version 1.0.0, 7.6 MB.

He runs **NIA: The Black Architecture Archive** at <https://nia.greenstoriesllc.com>. Architect profiles. Video interviews. Data on the field. Built by a designer in active practice, not by a historian after the fact.

## Story angles

A reporter can run with any of these. They don't compete with each other.

### 1. The 2,500
Fewer than 3,000 Black architects practice in the United States. About 2,500 of them are men. Tom Reynolds is on the path to becoming one of those men and has spent more than a decade building the on-ramp for the people behind him. The story is the on-ramp: the videos, the free tools, the archive, the refusal to wait for the institutional fix.

### 2. From Mack-Cali to TikTok
9 years on commercial office buildings. Then a smartphone. The story is what happens when a working designer with that much real-job experience starts publishing for free, in 60-second clips, to an audience of architecture students, contractors, and people who didn't know what an architect actually does.

### 3. AnansiBuild
A Revit plugin that exports BIM models to multi-color 3MF for 3D printing. 26 printers. 19 architectural scales. Auto material separation. Currently in private beta. The story is about a practitioner solving a workflow gap nobody else solved, named after the West African folk hero.

### 4. NIA: The Black Architecture Archive
A practitioner-built archive at nia.greenstoriesllc.com. Architect profiles, video interviews, data. The story is what changes about how a profession remembers itself when the people doing the documenting are still on jobsites.

### 5. The 22 tools
Footings, egress, fixtures, joists, code compliance. 22 free calculators built by an architectural designer who actually uses them. The story is about practice friction — what tools the industry has been quietly making people pay for, and what happens when one designer stops accepting that.
