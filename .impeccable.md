## Design Context

### Users
Aspiring architects, architecture students, young Black professionals considering the field, and the broader TikTok audience interested in architecture and representation. They arrive from TikTok or social media, on mobile or desktop, often casually browsing. The site is a personal brand hub — it needs to communicate credibility, mission, and personality quickly.

### Brand Personality
**Raw, authoritative, purposeful.** Tom Reynolds is an architectural designer who speaks plainly about the profession's diversity problem. The brand is not corporate or polished — it's direct, grounded in real work, and unapologetic. Think construction documents, not marketing brochures.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Brutalist construction-document.** The site is designed as an architectural "drawing set" — each section is a full-viewport "sheet" with its own background color, drafting grid overlay, corner coordinates, title blocks, and sheet numbers. The metaphor is deeply intentional: Tom is an architect, and the site itself is architected like a building project.

- **Typography**: Fraunces (variable serif, display), Archivo (sans, UI), JetBrains Mono (technical details)
- **Color palette**: Per-section color grounds — safety orange (vitals), blueprint cyan (tools), deep archive green (NIA), construction yellow (the game), concrete gray (AnansiBuild), bone/paper (bio), ink/dark (cover, videos, links, footer)
- **Theme**: Dark base with per-section color shifts. Not a typical dark mode — each section is its own world.
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS sites, glassmorphism, rounded cards, gradient text, typical "creator" link-in-bio pages

### Design Principles
1. **The metaphor is the design.** Every visual choice reinforces the construction-document concept — grids, stamps, redlines, title blocks, sheet numbers.
2. **Facts over decoration.** Content is presented as data: ledger grids, spec sheets, numbered indices. No filler.
3. **Each section earns its ground.** Every chapter has a distinct background color tied to its subject matter. Color is structural, not decorative.
4. **Hard edges, no apologies.** Zero border-radius. Offset box-shadow hovers. The aesthetic is brutalist and mechanical.
5. **Real voice only.** All copy comes from Tom's actual words and verified facts. No placeholder marketing language.
